About WHO

- Founded on April 7, 1948

- Parent Organization: United Nations Economic and Social Council

- WHO,which stands for World Health Organization, is a specialized agency of the United Nations. WHO is responsible for international public health. The WHO Constitution, which establishes the agency's governing structure and principles, states its main objective as "the attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health."
